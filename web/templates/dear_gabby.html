Dear Gabby

Deadpan advice for people who should know better

Dear Gabby,

My boyfriend keeps leaving his socks everywhere. On the couch, in the kitchen, sometimes in the refrigerator. I've tried everything - buying sock organizers, leaving notes, even throwing them away. He just buys more socks. What should I do?

- Sockless in Seattle

Dear Sockless,

Have you considered that your boyfriend is conducting a sophisticated behavioral experiment? The refrigerator socks suggest he's testing temperature effects on cotton blends. The couch socks are clearly studying fabric decay rates. This isn't a relationship problem - it's groundbreaking domestic science. Support his research and maybe co-author the paper.

- Gabby

Dear Gabby,

My coworker keeps bringing in "healthy" snacks and leaving them in the break room. Yesterday it was kale chips. Last week it was cauliflower popcorn. Nobody eats them. They just sit there judging our regular chips. How do I make this stop?

- Just Want Doritos

Dear Just Want,

This is clearly a performance art piece about workplace wellness culture. The uneaten snacks represent society's rejection of performative health consciousness. Your coworker is making a powerful statement about consumer choice and authenticity. You should write an art review and display it next to the snacks.

- Gabby

Dear Gabby,

Your cat is obviously preparing for a career in economics or political commentary. The meowing during fiscal policy discussions is sophisticated economic analysis. The hunger strike is a legitimate protest mechanism. You should set up a tiny podium and start recording his policy positions. This could be the next big political commentator.

- Gabby
